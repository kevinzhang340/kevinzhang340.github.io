About Me

I am a second year doctoral student in mathematics at the Courant Institute of New York University. I am interested in stochastic analysis and probability, specifically analyzing sampling algorithms and explaining the surprises in efficiency or effectiveness that arise in practice. I am also interested in novel solutions (e.g. self-similar) to partial differential equations, and the involvement of modern machine learning in finding such solutions.

I received my B.S. in Applied Mathematics at Columbia University School of Engineering (summa cum laude). My favorite Columbia CC books are the Structures of Scientific Revolution, Discourses on Livy, and Leisure, the Basis of Culture. In my free time, I enjoy tennis, volleyball, and classical music. Refreshing desserts and karaoke have been my favorite ways to relax after work.
